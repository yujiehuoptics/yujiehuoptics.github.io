A Comprehensive Investigation into Rapid Ghost Image Suppression Techniques in Spectral Domain Optical Coherence Tomography (SD-OCT)


Description:
Build the fiber based system and research on phase-shift methods
Time: Oct.2013 - Oct 2014

Introduction:
Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a technique that primarily uses a Michelson interferometer to collect information from samples. Frequency-domain optical coherence tomography obtains depth information of the sample through Fourier transform. However, the complex-conjugate artifact produced by the Fourier transform limits the detection depth. In this project, the main objective is to construct a frequency-domain optical coherence tomography system and a phase-shift demodulation system. Additionally, the research focuses on the phase-shift algorithms, aiming to investigate methods that reduce the number of phase shifts to eliminate the complex-conjugate artifact. This will lead to faster OCT scanning and improved imaging speed of the entire object.
